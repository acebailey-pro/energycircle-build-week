# EnergyCircle demo video

Target length: **2 minutes 40 seconds**. The contest limit is under three
minutes, so stop recording if the final edit reaches 2:55.

## Before recording

- Open the deployed EnergyCircle URL in a clean browser window.
- Set browser zoom to 100% and close unrelated tabs and notifications.
- Confirm Gravity storage is reset and begins **FRAGILE**.
- Practice the clicks below once without recording.
- Record at 1080p if available. Use a quiet room and speak naturally.
- Do not add copyrighted music, third-party logos, or unrelated browser content.

Windows options include the Snipping Tool screen recorder, Xbox Game Bar, or
Clipchamp. Record the screen and microphone together, then trim only the start,
end, and obvious pauses. The product—not editing effects—should carry the demo.

## Spoken script and actions

### 0:00–0:18 — The problem

**Say:**

> A property may already have sunlight, wind, water, elevation, heat, biomass,
> or human power—but understanding what those resources can actually do usually
> means searching through disconnected calculators and technical documents.
> EnergyCircle turns them into complete, understandable property systems.

**Show:** The opening property and the nine-family atlas.

**Then say:**

> I start with an objective, a realistic budget, and what the property may
> already offer. EnergyCircle compares all nine paths and explains each match;
> it does not choose for me. If a required resource is absent, the model cannot
> invent output. A possible but unmeasured resource is visibly derated.

**Do:** Change one objective and one resource, then pause on the top matches.

### 0:18–0:48 — One interaction proves the model

**Say:**

> This gravity-storage reference begins fragile. When I move the upper reservoir
> uphill, I am not moving a decorative icon. One governed state transition
> changes geometry, hydraulic head, pipe losses, stored energy, runtime,
> warnings, feasibility, and the plain-language explanation together.

**Do:** Drag the upper reservoir uphill. Pause on the changed verdict and
“What changed” explanation.

### 0:48–1:12 — Nine real system families

**Say:**

> EnergyCircle includes nine first-class governed families—not nine information
> cards. Solar photovoltaic, solar heat, wind, water and pressure, bioenergy,
> recovered heat, mechanical power, gravity storage, and coordinated hybrids
> each have their own components, variables, balance, failure path, and cost
> model.

**Do:** Open Solar PV, change solar-array size, then open Hybrid. Do not linger.

### 1:12–1:38 — Affordability and access

**Say:**

> A complete installation is not everyone's starting point. Every family has a
> five-stage access path—from using what already exists at zero new-equipment
> cost, through reclaimed, starter, and staged systems, to a complete reference
> system. EnergyCircle clearly distinguishes avoided demand or direct service
> from generated electricity, so it never markets a free whole-home system that
> physics cannot support.

**Do:** Open **$0 to complete** and scroll through the five levels.

### 1:38–2:12 — From exploration to field planning

**Say:**

> The model continues into a connected component schedule, itemized planning
> budget, resilience matrix, field sequence, and revision record. I can replace
> a component with a verified-reclaimed, standard-new, or high-duty option, and
> governed output, cost, assemblies, and exports follow. Exploded assemblies
> expose parts, interfaces, inspection gates, failure effects, service access,
> and isolation. Cost of inaction uses editable assumptions and time horizons,
> but explains the comparison instead of making the decision.

**Do:** Replace the selected component, open Exploded assemblies, briefly open
Cost of inaction, then show Print / save PDF.

### 2:12–2:34 — Codex and GPT-5.6

**Say:**

> I used Codex and GPT-5.6 as my implementation partner during Build Week. They
> accelerated product analysis, deterministic engine implementation, interface
> development, accessibility, provenance review, and automated tests across all
> nine families. I made the governing product decisions: AI may explain the
> result, but it cannot invent physical values or override the deterministic
> energy balance.

**Show:** Return briefly to the interactive result and truth-state labels.

### 2:34–2:46 — Close

**Say:**

> EnergyCircle helps someone move from “my property may have energy potential”
> to “I understand the system, its limits, its cost, what to measure next, and
> where professional review begins.” EnergyCircle: property energy systems, made
> legible.

**Show:** EnergyCircle brand and active system.
